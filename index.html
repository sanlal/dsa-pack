Recommended order

The 6-step path

If you're starting fresh, this is the order that works. If you only have a week, do the OOP Notes first, then drill the Top 50.

  1. Begin with the OOP Notes. Object-Oriented thinking is the mental model the rest of the pack assumes. Includes SOLID + the 7 must-know design patterns — everything you'll need before System Design.
  2. Move on to DSA Fundamentals. Skim every chapter so you know what tools exist. Don't memorise — just see them.
  3. Learn the 14 Patterns. These are the templates. 90% of coding questions are a variation of one of these.
  4. Drill the Top 50 Problems. Practise each one in your weakest language. Re-do the ones you got wrong after a week.
  5. Work through System Design. LLD first (it leans on the OOP you just learned), then HLD. Practise the URL shortener, chat, and feed walkthroughs out loud.
  6. Finish with the Interview Playbook. Soft skills, HR prep, the 60-day schedule and salary negotiation — the layer that actually closes offers.
